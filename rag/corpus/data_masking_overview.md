# What Is Data Masking

Data masking replaces sensitive production values with protected values that keep the same business shape. Enterprises use it when developers, analysts, testers, or support teams need realistic data without direct access to personal or regulated information.

Masking is commonly applied to customer names, email addresses, phone numbers, national identifiers, payment numbers, IP addresses, and dates of birth. A good masking workflow protects privacy while preserving referential integrity and enough format detail for downstream systems to keep working.

Use masking when copying production data into lower environments, sharing datasets with partners, training support teams, or preparing demos. The goal is to reduce exposure, not to replace access control, consent management, or retention policies.
