# GDPR and CCPA Compliance

Data masking helps reduce exposure of personal data in non-production workflows. For GDPR and CCPA programs, masking should be combined with purpose limitation, access controls, audit logging, retention controls, and documented review.

For customer data, mask direct identifiers such as names, email addresses, phone numbers, payment identifiers, postal addresses, IP addresses, and national IDs before copying data to development, testing, analytics, or partner environments.

Compliance teams should review masking policy exceptions. The system should log who approved rules, which columns were masked, which columns required review, and when each job ran.
